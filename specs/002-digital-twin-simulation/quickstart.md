# Quickstart: Digital Twin Simulation for Embodied AI

## Overview
This module introduces intermediate AI and Python learners to digital twin simulation for embodied AI, focusing on physics simulation, environment modeling, and sensor perception. You'll learn how simulated worlds train and validate AI-driven humanoid robots, with an emphasis on sim2real workflows that connect simulation to real-world deployment.

## Prerequisites
- Intermediate understanding of AI/ML concepts
- Basic Python knowledge
- Familiarity with system architecture concepts
- No prior simulation or robotics experience required

## Getting Started

### 1. Chapter 1: Physics Simulation Foundations
Start with understanding the fundamentals of physics simulation including gravity, collisions, and constraints.
- Learn how simulated environments model real-world physics
- Understand the limitations of physics simulation compared to reality
- Explore the balance between modeling fidelity and computational complexity

### 2. Chapter 2: Environment Design & Interaction
Explore how virtual worlds are designed for robot training and human-robot interaction concepts.
- Study virtual world design for specific training purposes
- Understand human-robot interaction modeling in simulation
- Learn about environment complexity and computational efficiency

### 3. Chapter 3: Sensor Simulation & Perception
Discover how sensors like LiDAR, depth cameras, and IMUs are simulated with realistic noise and latency.
- Understand sensor simulation models and their limitations
- Learn about noise, latency, and realism in sensor data
- Explore the sim2real transfer process for sensor systems

## Learning Path
1. Begin with Chapter 1 to establish physics simulation foundations
2. Progress to Chapter 2 to understand environment design and interaction
3. Complete with Chapter 3 to grasp sensor simulation and perception
4. Review all takeaways to consolidate understanding of sim2real workflows

## Expected Outcomes
After completing this module, you will be able to:
- Explain physics simulation concepts (gravity, collisions, constraints)
- Understand how virtual environments are designed for robot training
- Describe sensor simulation for LiDAR, depth cameras, and IMUs
- Grasp sim2real concepts and challenges
- Trace the path from sensors → perception → AI behavior
